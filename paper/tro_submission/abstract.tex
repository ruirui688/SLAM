Long-term visual SLAM in industrial environments faces a bottleneck that neither geometric SLAM nor object-level semantic mapping directly addresses: which detected objects deserve durable map slots, and which should be rejected as transient or dynamic contamination? Open-vocabulary detection pipelines produce candidate object instances from every frame, but naively inserting all geometrically consistent detections contaminates the map with forklifts, carts, and single-visit phantoms---a dynamic contamination problem recognized in dynamic SLAM literature~\cite{bescos2018dynaslam} but not resolved at the semantic map-admission level. This paper formulates \textbf{session-level map admission control} as a distinct contribution: an explicit object-maintenance layer between perception and the map that transforms open-vocabulary RGB-D segmentation outputs into observation, tracklet, map-object, and revision layers, then applies boolean criteria (multi-session presence, minimum observation support, label consistency, static dominance) to admit stable semantic landmarks and reject dynamic contamination. We evaluate the framework on the TorWIC dataset~\cite{barath2023povslam}, reporting a reproducible evidence ladder across same-day (203 observations / 11 candidate clusters $\rightarrow$ 5 retained), cross-day (240/10$\rightarrow$5), and cross-month (297/14$\rightarrow$7) Aisle protocols, with a separate Hallway within-site variation branch (537/16$\rightarrow$9). Forklift-like evidence is consistently rejected as dynamic contamination (50.0\%--71.4\% rejection share across all four protocols). We validate the admission policy through parameter ablation across 27 combinations and baseline comparison against naive-all-admit and purity/support heuristics, which cannot reject forklifts (phantom risk~20.0\%$\rightarrow$0.0\% with the full policy, McNemar $p{<}0.001$). We also report the current dataset-mask-supervised dynamic/non-static front-end route: 237 rows from 79 TorWIC/AnnotatedSemanticSet frame groups with zero split overlap, a compact CUDA UNet reaching validation/test IoU-F1 of 0.671304/0.803329 and 0.578580/0.733038, six held-out mask samples with mean precision/recall/F1/IoU of 0.556007/0.789669/0.604636/0.443210, and an ORB proxy reducing keypoints inside ground-truth dynamic regions from 4795 to 2192 (54.2857\%). This learned component is a front-end mask predictor, not learned persistent-map admission control. The contribution is bounded: not a lifelong SLAM benchmark, not dense dynamic reconstruction, not learned admission control, and not a downstream navigation or ATE/RPE improvement claim.
